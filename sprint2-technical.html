Margins, Borders, and Padding

Margins, borders, and padding give your content space and help with positioning it on your webpage.

Box Model - The Gallery

The Box Model refers to how your content is designed and laid out on the screen. Think of your content as always being in boxes. The 'boxes', better known as margins, borders, and padding, are able to be manipulated in various ways allowing you to have an effect on your webpage layouts.

Think of your webpage as a gallery wall. An artist would make decisions about how their work is laid out in the gallery. To do this, they would bring their art as well as tools to help them decide on the best layout. Just like the artist, you will be making decisions about how your content is laid out on your webpage. Let's Dive Deeper

An artist considers the placement and framing of their work before they bring it into a gallery to display it. You will also need to think about the placement of your content on a webpage. Margins, borders, and padding will assist you in that visual placement of your content.

  • Margins are the distance between two elements. Think of it like the spacing between photographs on a wall. You will also want to have spaces between content on your webpage.
  • Borders go around the content. Think of these like a picture frame you would put a photograph into.
    (Borders are not required to be used on a webpage but for the purposes of this explanation we will be using borders.)
  • Padding is the distance between the border and the inner content. Again, if you are to think of a framed photograph there would be the frame then the inner padding, followed by the photo itself.
By utilising these concepts you allow the eye to be drawn to the inner content whilst giving it breathing room to sing.

Using the Elements

You will now have a better understanding of how margins, borders, and padding relate to one another. But how would you write this in CSS?

Individual margins would be written into your CSS code using the following format:

  • margin-top: 10px;
  • margin-right: 10px;
  • margin-bottom: 10px;
  • margin-left: 10px;
Or you could write it short hand as one line:
  • margin: 10px 10px 10px 10px;

Individual padding sides would be written into your CSS code using the following format:

  • padding-top: 10px;
  • padding-right: 10px;
  • padding-bottom: 10px;
  • padding-left: 10px;
Or you could write it short hand as one line:
  • padding: 10px 10px 10px 10px;
Your code can be written in pixels (px) or percentages (%). An important thing to remember if you are writing code in one line is that the adjustments will always go by the following structure: top, right, bottom, left.
